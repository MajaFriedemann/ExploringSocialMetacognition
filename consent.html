Exploring Social Metacognition
Participant information
updated 13 June 2018

Welcome. Below you will find information that will help you to decide whether to take part in this study. If you have questions about anything, please feel free to contact us.

This study aims to discover how your knowledge of your own thoughts affects your social world, and how your social world affects your thoughts. This will help us understand how social networks are constructed, how information flows within them, and how they affect people's lives.

In the study you will perform computer-based tasks in which you have to make decisions and use different types of advice. At various points in the study you will be asked to fill in questionnaires about yourself or your advisors. The study will take approximately 1 hour.

You are free to withdraw from the study at any point. To do so, simply close your browser tab. There will be no penalty for doing so. Data for some studies are collected continuall. We are unlikely to be able to remove collected data from analysis.

We collect data on your performance during the study. Upon completion of the study you will be given feedback on your performance, and provided with a link to retrieve this feedback. You may share this link at your discretion. We will make no attempt to identify you, or link your performance to any personal data we hold. Anonymised data and results from the study will form part of a doctoral thesis, will be made accessible to the wider scientific community, and may be published in peer-reviewed scientific journals and presented at scientific conferences.

Personal data, specifically IP addresses, are logged by the server as part of the normal process of providing secure and stable service. The University of Oxford is the data controller with respect to these data. Your personal data are stored separately and will not be linked to your performance. Your personal data will not be shared, and only responsible members of the University of Oxford will be allowed access for monitoring, audit, or server administration purposes.

Payment rates and conditions, where applicable, will have been made clear previously. If you are unsure about payment, please contact us.

Ethical approval for this study has been granted by the Oxford Central University Research Ethics Committee (CUREC), reference R55382_RE003. There are no significant risks or benefits to you, although the data will contribute to scientific discovery.

If you have a concern about any aspect of this project, please speak to the researcher, Matt Jaquiery [matt.jaquiery@psy.ox.ac.uk], or the principal investigator, Prof Nick Yeung [nicholas.yeung@psy.ox.ac.uk], who will do their best to answer your query. The researcher should acknowledge your concern within 10 working days and give you an indication of how they intend to deal with it. If you remain unhappy or wish to make a formal complaint, please contact the Chair of the Research Ethics Committee at the University of Oxford: Chair, Medical Sciences Inter-Divisional Research Ethics Committee; Email: ethics@medsci.ox.ac.uk; Address: Research Services, University of Oxford, Wellington Square, Oxford OX1 2JD.

The study is funded by the Attention and Cognitive Control Laboratory at Oxford University, and by grant #661552 from the Medical Research Council .
